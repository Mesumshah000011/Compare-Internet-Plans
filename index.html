What is an NBN Plan?

Before diving into comparisons, let's talk about the NBN (National Broadband Network). It's Australia's high-speed broadband network, and most providers use it to offer internet plans. These NBN plans are divided into speed tiers.

Common NBN Speed Tiers in 2025

  • NBN 25 – Good for singles or small households (Basic browsing and email)
  • NBN 50 – Great for families with moderate streaming needs (HD video, social media)
  • NBN 100 – Ideal for 4K streaming and online gaming (Multiple devices)
  • NBN 250 & 1000 – Super-fast speeds for heavy internet users (Large households, 4K streaming on multiple devices)

Key Things to Check When You Compare Internet Plans

Not all internet plans are built the same. Look closely at these factors:

1

Monthly Price

The first thing we all notice is the price tag. But don't jump on the cheapest option without checking for setup costs or modem fees.

2

Speed

A plan can be cheap but painfully slow. Balance speed and cost. For example, a family of four will likely need at least an NBN 50 plan.

3

Data Limits

Most plans now offer unlimited data. But a few budget options still have caps. Always check this before signing up.

4

Contract Terms

Prefer flexibility? Go for no-lock-in contracts. Switching early can be costly with fixed-term plans.

Pro Tip: Look Beyond the Price Tag!

Consider extras like free streaming subscriptions, mobile bundles, or energy discounts that can add significant value to your plan.

How to Compare Internet Plans Easily

Doing an NBN plan compare doesn't have to be hard. Here's a simple approach:

4-Step Plan Comparison Process

  1. Check Your Usage - Do you stream Netflix or YouTube a lot? Do you need faster speeds for remote work or gaming?
  2. Decide on Your Budget - Figure out how much you're comfortable spending each month.
  3. Use Comparison Tools - Plenty of websites allow you to compare internet plans side by side.
  4. Look for Deals - Many providers offer discounted rates for the first 6 months.

NBN Plan Compare for Families vs. Singles

Your choice depends on how many people are online at the same time.

  • Singles or Couples: NBN 25 or NBN 50 is usually enough. Prices start at $55–$65 per month.
  • Families: Go for NBN 100 or higher. Expect to pay around $80/month or more.

Tips to Save Money on Internet Plans

  • Switch Providers: Many companies offer big discounts for new customers.
  • Bundle Services: Pair internet with mobile or energy for extra savings.
  • Skip Speeds You Don't Need: NBN 50 works well for most users.
  • Negotiate: Call your current provider and ask for a better deal.

FAQs About Internet Plans in 2025

How often should I compare internet plans?

It's smart to check every 6–12 months since providers often refresh their deals.

Is NBN 50 enough for a family of four?

Yes, it works for most families unless everyone streams in 4K at the same time.

Do I need a contract to get the best price?

No. Many no-lock-in plans now offer competitive prices without tying you down.

Which provider is the cheapest in 2025?

Tangerine and Dodo are among the cheapest, starting around $60 per month.
